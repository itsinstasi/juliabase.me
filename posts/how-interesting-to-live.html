How interesting to live! So many life interventions and stories happen.
Here is the story, friends ...
I was born in the Crimea, in Sevastopol, I lived there until almost 20 years. I love these places. This is my homeland. I've been living in different cities and countries for many years. I travel a lot ...
In those early times, when everyone came to Yalta for the summer, I was very proud of being a Crimean woman and living in such a beautiful place. My grandmother, aunt and sister (loved ones❤️) are from Yalta. Therefore, most of the time we spent there, on the Southern Coast of Crimea🌊☀️. Regular exits to the sea on a yacht🛥, trips to the Swallow's Nest, to the Alupkinsky and Vorontsov palaces🕌. Sun ☀, sea 🌊 abundance of fruits 🍇 and later champagne🥂 🍾 (our Crimean😊) ..
So I lived a splendid life. Even skipping lessons, we ran to the beach to swim and sunbathe😆. And in the autumn we drove with the whole class to collect grapes 🍇 and petals of the Crimean rose🌹.
Everything was available and close to each other. The best children's camps and boarding houses-Izumrud, Batiliman, Livadia, Oreanda are native and favorite names..
The only thing that was very close, but at the same time equated to the availability of space 🚀- Artek. Artek was and is a sign of quality, an object of prestige and an order for merit! And getting into there was simply unrealistic. Special and very talented children from all over the country spent time there. I could only dream of being there, which I did.
And today our eldest son flew to the summer shift in Artek !!!🤯🤩
It's hard to believe, but it's a fact.
So, friends, the moral of the story is the following-nothing impossible.
We must do, live, love, open ourselves to the world and people. And all dreams will come true !!!!🥂✌🏼
